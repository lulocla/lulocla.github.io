ELE024 Mythbusters: Food Fact, Fads and Fiction

Description

This module gave me an understanding of some of the processes involved in food production, and included debunking myths of the food industry, discussing common misconceptions and exploring ideas which present the food and nutritional industries in a bad light.

Aims

The aim of this Module is to provide the student with: knowledge and understanding of the different aspects of the food industry and their impacts on our lives.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module I was able to:

  1. Discern current information and identify common misconceptions regarding where our food and drink comes from and how
  2. Appraise current data within the context of the food and drink industry, and reflect on their impact on society
  3. Present in a team environment a reflective account of an aspect from the module
Project
Should we tax unhealthy food and drink? (Presentation).
Key Transferable Skills for this module are:

Communication, Enterprise, Team Work, Problem Solving, Research, ICT Skills, Planning, Interpersonal, Professionalism

Indicative content
  1. Student led investigations

    2. Student led investigations of the different sectors within the food and drink industry including: prebiotics - and the controversy surrounding the term; if barbequing is a healthy cooking method; and exposing the celebrity chef - common Myths about cooking

  2. Consumerism⁠

    3. Does the food industry listen to us? -Understanding consumer and sensory science to better understand why you buy the products you buy. - How food / public health is reported by the media? - The French paradox / Mediterranean diet.

  3. ⁠Future of food ⁠

    4. Ethical food production and the future of foods, and, what-s waste got to do with it?

  4. Debunking myths

    5. Debunking food myths, more science than science fiction in our food today, like the science behind getting sauce out of a bottle and what to drink - Whisky or Beer?

  5. Facts from fiction

    6. Finding facts from fiction, investigating the three second rule - should I eat things that have fallen on the floor? What-s so super about super-foods? Fat or sugar: Which is worse?
