Graduate Award Plus

Description

This module focuses on developing essential employability skills and provides guidance on managing graduate interviews and applications. The module is designed to support students in their final year as they transition into graduate employment or further studies. The module is divided into two parts:

  • Part 1: Employability Skills That MatterThis section explores employability skills in detail from an employer’s perspective, with a particular emphasis on Emotional Intelligence. A recent survey of graduate employers ranked Emotional Intelligence as the second most important skill for the future workforce.
  • Part 2: Managing Interviews and ApplicationsThis section aims to enhance students' understanding of Networking and offers strategies for effective conversations with potential employers.

Aims

The aim of this module is to support students in focusing on their career goals after university while managing their studies in their final year. It helps students develop crucial employability skills, understand the importance of Emotional Intelligence, and improve their networking abilities.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, I was able to:

  1. Understand and articulate the employability skills valued by employers
  2. Recognize the significance of Emotional Intelligence in the workplace.
  3. Develop effective networking strategies and communication skills with potential employers
  4. Prepare for graduate interviews and applications with a comprehensive understanding of employer expectations.
Indicative content

Core Elements of the GAP Programme

  1. Graduate CV: Development of a CV tailored to the student's industry/sector.
  2. LinkedIn Profile: Creation and development of a professional LinkedIn profile.
  3. Career Module 1:
    • Part 1: Employability Skills That Matter
    • Part 2: Managing Interviews and Applications
Key Transferable Skills for this module are:

Employability Skills: Detailed exploration of skills from an employer’s perspective.

Emotional Intelligence: Importance and application in the workplace.

Networking Skills: Techniques for effective networking and employer engagement.

Interview and Application Management: Strategies for successful graduate interviews and applications.


This structured approach ensures that students are well-prepared for the transition from academic life to graduate employment or further studies.
