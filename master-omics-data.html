Omics Data Analysis

Compulsory  |  5 ECTS  |  Code: M0.157  |  18 Feb 2026 – 1 Jul 2026

Professor: Alex Sánchez Pla

About this Subject

Molecular biology has generated enormous amounts of data from sequencing projects and gene expression technologies. This module covers the statistical and bioinformatic methods needed to process, analyse, and interpret high-throughput omics data — from microarrays and RNA-seq to proteomics, metabolomics, and multi-omics integration. Special emphasis is placed on R and Bioconductor for complete analysis pipelines.

Contents
  • I. The Omics (30%) — introduction to omics, HTS methods, Bioconductor, Galaxy, omics data analysis process, PCA, clustering
  • II. Gene Expression Analysis (40%) — microarray and RNA-seq data processing, quality control, normalisation, batch effect correction, differential expression (limma, DESeq2), multiple testing, gene list analysis (ORA, GSEA)
  • III. Other Omics Analyses (30%) — DNA-seq variant selection and annotation, metabolomics, proteomics, epigenomics (methylation), metagenomics, multi-omics integration, single-cell analysis
Skills Gained
  • Microarray and RNA-seq data processing with Bioconductor
  • Differential expression analysis (limma, DESeq2, edgeR)
  • Batch effect detection and correction
  • Gene ontology and pathway enrichment analysis (ORA, GSEA)
  • Genomic variant selection and annotation with Galaxy
  • Multi-omics and single-cell data integration
