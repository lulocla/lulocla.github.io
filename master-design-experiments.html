Design & Analysis of Experiments

Optional  |  5 ECTS  |  Code: M0.162  |  18 Feb 2026 – 1 Jul 2026

Professor: Ferran Reverter Comes, Jose Luis Mosquera Mayo

About this Subject

Experimentation is intrinsic to most scientific and technological research. This module teaches how to design experiments that correctly identify the factors influencing a variable of interest and estimate their effect, minimising confounding due to sample variability. A well-designed experiment is determined by the choice of experimental units, treatments, and assignment mechanism. Applications span biotechnology, clinical trials, industrial processes, ecology, and medicine.

Contents
  • Unit 1: Introduction — components of an experiment, experimental units, response variables, randomisation
  • Unit 2: Completely randomised design — statistical model, ANOVA, contrasts
  • Unit 3: Multiple comparisons — Bonferroni methods, pairwise comparisons
  • Unit 4: Model assumption checking — normality, constant variance, independence
  • Unit 5: Power and sample size — methodologies for sample size selection, power in ANOVA
  • Unit 6: Factorial models — main effects, interactions, balanced ANOVA
  • Unit 7: Random effects — random effect designs, variance components, mixed models
  • Unit 8: Nested factor models — nested and crossed factors, Bennett-Franklin rules
  • Unit 9: Block designs and covariates — randomised complete block design, ANCOVA
  • Unit 10: Introduction to linear mixed models
Skills Gained
  • Experimental design principles and randomisation
  • ANOVA — one-way, factorial, block designs
  • Multiple comparison methods (Bonferroni, pairwise)
  • Model assumption verification and correction
  • Power analysis and sample size calculation
  • Linear mixed models and ANCOVA
