Abertay University Career Mentoring Scheme

Aims

The Abertay University Career Mentoring Scheme aimed to connect final and penultimate year students with local professionals, providing them with an additional opportunity to enhance their personal and employability skills. Simultaneously, the scheme enabled professionals to undergo personal and professional development.

Purpose of the Career Mentoring Scheme

The Career Mentoring Scheme at Abertay University served two main objectives: raising aspirations and enhancing employability:

  1. Aspirational Objective: To elevate students’ ambitions and broaden horizons concerning career paths, learning goals, and self-awareness.
  2. Employability Objective: To foster the development of knowledge, skills, and personal qualities valued by employers.
Role of the Mentee

The mentee was an active collaborator in the mentoring relationship, engaging in learning and critical reflection on experiences. It was crucial for the mentee to take charge of the process, communicate goals and expectations clearly, and actively seek information and guidance. The role of the mentee included:

  • Asking questions
  • Being open to mentoring
  • Striving for excellence
  • Accepting constructive criticism graciously
  • Learning from mistakes
  • Having the courage to try new things
  • Accepting responsibilities
  • Being open, honest, respectful, and appreciative
  • Listening, observing, learning, and growing
Role of the Mentor

The mentor-student relationship was student-centered, focusing on the mentee’s professional and personal development. The mentor provided informal advice, different perspectives, support, encouragement, and empowered the student to take charge of their own development. The role of the mentor included:

  • Listening
  • Providing informal advice on career development
  • Offering different perspectives
  • Offering support and encouragement
  • Questioning to elicit information
  • Drawing on personal experiences where appropriate
  • Empowering the student to set goals and complete action plans
  • Taking the lead and making suggestions, especially in the early stages of the relationship
Mentor’s Role in Encouraging the Student

Mentors encouraged students to:

  • Raise aspirations
  • Develop employability skills
  • Review and reflect on themselves
  • Listen and clarify understanding
  • Consider different perspectives
  • Challenge/change assumptions
  • Manage their career path
  • Take responsibility for personal and professional decisions
  • Make decisions and achieve goals to maximize the outcomes of the mentoring relationship
