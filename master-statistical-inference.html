Statistical Inference

Compulsory  |  5 ECTS  |  Code: M0.155  |  18 Feb 2026 – 1 Jul 2026

Professor: Alex Sánchez Pla, Santiago Pérez Hoyos

About this Subject

Statistics is the science concerned with the collection, modelling and analysis of data in the presence of uncertainty. This module focuses on statistical inference — drawing conclusions about a population from representative samples using probabilistic models. It builds the conceptual foundations needed for all other modules in the master's, covering probability, estimation, hypothesis testing, and computational methods.

Contents
  • Unit I: Probability and random variables — probabilistic models in biology, Bayes' theorem, diagnostic test evaluation
  • Unit II: Random variables and distributions — expectation, variance, notable probability distributions, exponential family
  • Unit III: Multidimensional distributions — joint/marginal/conditional distributions, covariance, central limit theorem
  • Unit IV: Sampling and estimation — likelihood, maximum likelihood and Bayesian estimators, bootstrap introduction
  • Unit V: Confidence intervals — classical and bootstrap methods for proportions, rates, odds ratios
  • Unit VI: Hypothesis testing — error types, significance, power, sample size calculations
  • Unit VII: Applied tests — normality tests, t-test, ANOVA, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis, chi-squared, Fisher's test
  • Unit VIII: Intensive computation — permutation tests, bootstrap, multiple comparisons (FWER, FDR)
Skills Gained
  • Probability rules and probabilistic modelling in biology
  • Maximum likelihood and Bayesian estimation
  • Confidence interval construction (classical and bootstrap)
  • Hypothesis testing — parametric and non-parametric
  • Multiple testing corrections (Bonferroni, FDR)
  • Permutation tests and computational inference in R
